ANSELM — Free Browser Strategy Game, No Download Required

ANSELM is a free, self-contained HTML5 browser game that requires no download, no server, no framework, and no installation. Open the single HTML file in any modern browser and play immediately. Built entirely with vanilla JavaScript, HTML Canvas, and the Web Audio API. Over 15,900 lines of code in one file. Version 3.7.0 — the Daedalus Codex Update.

The Story

You are the Traveller. You exist between dimensions. Five parallel realms are dying, consumed by a sentient black corruption called the Goo that spreads outward from a hidden Nexus Eye in each world. You are the only being capable of crossing between dimensions via the Gateway. Your mission is to nurture each realm's indigenous civilisation, forge realm-specific weapons from MSM Crystals, and destroy the Nexus Eye in all five realms before the darkness consumes everything.

ANSELM is melancholic, mythic, and quietly hopeful. Its world is grounded in a sense of cosmic weight drawn from the in-game text Lux in Tenebris — Light in Darkness — an ancient book the natives study as they advance through the ages.

Gameplay Features

The Chamber Room and Daedalus Codex

New in version 3.7.0, the game opens in the Chamber — a small stone room lit by wall torches. A tall machine called the Daedalus stands at the far end. Power it with a Pink Crystal to unlock a six-screen briefing covering the Goo, the natives, the weapons system, and your mission. The exit to Ashveil only opens once the briefing is complete. Inside the Chamber, a fully procedural recreation of the Fairy Fountain theme plays — D major, 6/8 time, 52 BPM, five simultaneous voice layers, room echo on every note. No audio file exists anywhere in the package.

The Daedalus Codex is an in-game encyclopedia accessible from the Chambers tile during gameplay and from the title screen between sessions. Seven chapters cover Natives, Dimensions, The Forge, Mining, Genetics, Civilisation, and Lux in Tenebris. The title-screen Codex shows which scripture chapters have been studied across all previous sessions, and displays the last six named artifacts forged in the world.

Genetics Engine

Every native carries ten genetic traits: agility, work ethic, social skill, faith, resilience, curiosity, hair colour, skin colour, home dimension, and gender. When two compatible natives mate near a shrine, offspring inherit traits 50/50 from each parent with a 15 percent chance of random mutation. Cross-dimensional couples produce hybrid children tinted with both realm colours. Corrupted natives pass Goo-spread traits to offspring, creating a biological threat that compounds over generations.

Civilisation Ages

Each realm progresses independently through five base ages: Primitive, Settlement, Town, City, and Heaven. Beyond Heaven, the Extended Age system draws from Lux in Tenebris: the Possession Age, the Shared Age, the Bandwidth Age, the Feed Field Age, the Hyper-Holo-Grid Age, and the On and On. At Age 8, realms establish Dimensional Contact. When all five connect, the Grid Awakening triggers. At Age 9, all active wars in a realm automatically end — the text names this moment the hyper-holo-grid achieving peace.

World Events

Every 2.5 to 5 minutes, a random world event may fire in any dimension. Meteor strikes form rock craters with Silverstone at the centre and scatter nearby natives. Dimensional storms accelerate civilisation XP for 10 seconds, with the Purple Realm gaining the largest bonus. The Great Migration sends wanderer-personality natives across dimensions, carrying their genetics with them. Eclipses throw all animals into flight mode and darken the realm briefly. These events are logged in the Chronicle panel when they occur in the player's current dimension.

Edict System

Once 20 loyalty points are accumulated in a dimension, the player may issue a royal Edict lasting five minutes. The Build Decree forces construction every five seconds. The Study Decree doubles scripture interpretation speed. Militarize lowers the army loyalty threshold from 3 to 2, recruiting more defenders immediately. The Expansion Edict triples migration speed across all dimensions. Only one Edict can be active per dimension at a time, and any Edict can be cancelled early.

Town Founding

The player can found new settlements anywhere in the active dimension by pressing F while standing on open ground. Nearby loyal natives must collectively carry at least 30 wood and 20 stone. When requirements are met, the resources are consumed, a Great Hall is placed, four huts are raised in cardinal directions, and four pairs of native settlers spawn. The new town is assigned a specialty — Farming, Mining, Trade, or Craft — and its founding is recorded in the Chronicle.

Diplomacy

Once two dimensions reach Age 8 and establish Dimensional Contact, their relationship begins to evolve. Realms with high affinity accumulate toward an alliance, which triggers migration bonuses between them. Low-affinity realms drift toward rivalry, and at high rivalry strength, raid parties of enemy-team natives begin appearing in the player's dimension. The Diplomacy panel, opened with D, allows the player to spend Refined Silverstone to broker peace or accelerate alliance bonuses. Two hate-pairs are hard-coded — Blue and Green, Green and Purple — and tend toward early conflict.

Realm Tension and War

Each dimension tracks a Tension meter from 0 to 100, rising when trees are depleted, resources run low, disputes are active, or Goo coverage grows. When Tension exceeds 70, the realm escalates through five war levels: Peace, Skirmish, Conflict, War, and Total War. At War level 3 and above, raid parties spawn periodically. At Total War, those parties may carry bombs. The Chronicle records every escalation. Reaching the Hyper-Holo-Grid Age automatically ends all wars in that realm, representing the civilisation transcending conflict entirely.

Meta-Progression

Between sessions, four pieces of data persist in the browser's local storage. The three highest-generation natives from any run carry their full trait sets into the next game, seeding the starting population with bloodlines that have already been shaped by generations of play. Dimensions that reached Heaven grant bonus starting XP and spawn Returner natives in the next run. Lux in Tenebris chapter progress persists, so the title-screen Codex fills in over multiple playthroughs. When a weapon is forged with third-generation or higher natives present, a native's name is attached to the weapon — Greyonnen's Tidecaller, Vaelthar's Ashen Blade — and those named artifacts accumulate in the Codex across all future sessions.

Native Quests

Every 80 seconds, a named neutral native in any dimension may offer a quest. Stone delivery quests ask for five Silverstone in exchange for town happiness and loyalty. Escort quests ask the player to stay close for 30 seconds, after which the native permanently joins at loyalty 3. Travel quests ask the player to leave the dimension and return, after which the native becomes a permanent trader and the dimension gains 20 XP. Only one quest per dimension can be active at a time.

The Goo

The Goo is not a character. It is a force. It pulses from its Nexus Eye every 20 to 50 seconds, spreading 8 to 15 tiles at a time. It converts ground tiles to corruption, kills animals on contact, and turns neutral natives into hostile chasers if they stand on corrupted ground too long. The HUD shows Goo coverage as a percentage with a heartbeat sound that accelerates as coverage rises. At high coverage the screen border pulses red. Fight back with MSM Crystals forged into weapons, Time Crystals planted to grow MSM crops, or the Ultimate Weapon to walk directly onto the Nexus Eye and destroy it.

The Forge and Weapons

The Forge sits at the exact centre of every map inside a Sacred Zone that nothing can overwrite. Five realm weapons are forged from MSM Crystals: the Ashen Blade from Ashveil, the Tidecaller from Thalassorn, the Verdant Spear from Verdekai, the Duskbrand from Solenthar, and the Voidreaver from Umbrakor. Combine all five weapons into the Ultimate Weapon. Time Crystals can now be planted on clear ground tiles and bloom into a 3x3 MSM crop after 60 seconds. When third-generation natives witness a forging, the weapon is named after their bloodline and recorded in the Codex.

Procedural Everything

All maps are generated from seeded noise with distinct terrain thresholds per realm. All music is generated live via the Web Audio API — no audio files exist in the package. All pixel art is drawn with fillRect calls. The Chamber ambient track is a full OoT-accurate Fairy Fountain reconstruction in D major. The main title theme is a 43-bar composed piece in A minor at 56 BPM using six distinct voice timbres with echo delays.

Natives and Loyalty

Each dimension begins with an indigenous population colour-coded to their realm. Natives have a rich AI state machine: idle, gather, carry resources, build huts, plant trees, trade between towns, hunt animals, sail between piers, sleep at campfires at night, escort the player, and in version 3.7.0, pursue quests. Walk near natives to raise their loyalty from neutral through ally to devoted. Devoted natives display gold crown dots, defend the Forge, and pass their best genetic traits to offspring.

The Five Realms

Realm 1 — Ashveil, the Pink Realm

Tutorial realm with no environmental hazard. Home of the Ashfawn, a deer-like creature. Boss: the Void Leviathan. Forge weapon: the Ashen Blade. Pink MSM Crystal doubles Silverstone yield. Rose-tinted meadows, gentle terrain, the first place the Goo awakens. The Chamber of First Light is set here — the Daedalus machine awaits before you ever set foot on its soil.

Realm 2 — Thalassorn, the Blue Realm

Flooding and stepping stones create a hazardous water environment. Home of the Driftray, a manta ray-like creature. Boss: the Crystal Behemoth, an armoured giant of ice that rose when the Nexus Eye formed. Forge weapon: the Tidecaller. Blue Crystal accelerates native mating. Hard-coded rival of Verdekai — tension builds here early.

Realm 3 — Verdekai, the Green Realm

Dense ancient forest with no additional hazard. Home of the Mosscrawler, an armadillo-like creature. Boss: the Storm Wyrm, a serpent of living storm and root, patient and poisonous. Forge weapon: the Verdant Spear. Green Crystal accelerates tree regrowth. Rivals both Thalassorn and Umbrakor by hard-coded affinity — the dimension most likely to be at war.

Realm 4 — Solenthar, the Beige Realm

Desert environment with sandstorm visibility hazard that reduces vision to nothing. Navigate by memory. Home of the Dustleaper, a rabbit-like creature. Boss: the Bone Colossus, assembled from the remains of all who fell to the Goo. Forge weapon: the Duskbrand.

Realm 5 — Umbrakor, the Purple Realm

Gravity drift hazard slowly pulls the player in a rotating direction. Home of the Voidmoth, a moth-like creature. Boss: the Shadow Titan, a creature of pure collapsed darkness that cannot be seen until it chooses to be. Forge weapon: the Voidreaver. Purple Crystal reveals the hidden Nexus Eye location. Dimensional storms here grant 75 percent more XP than in other realms.

Lux in Tenebris

Lux in Tenebris, meaning Light in Darkness, is an ancient in-game text that seeds all post-Heaven civilisational development. Its ten chapters correspond to ten principles: Mind, Body, Soul, Self, Order, Life, Time, Eternity, Death, and Repeat. Each chapter flavours a new civilisational era. Scholars in each realm study successive chapters as they advance through the ages, interpreting ancient wisdom to build new wonders. Chapter progress now persists across sessions in the Daedalus Codex, accumulating over multiple playthroughs.

Technical Details

ANSELM runs in a single self-contained HTML file using vanilla JavaScript, HTML Canvas 2D, and the Web Audio API. No frameworks, no dependencies, no server. Uses seeded Lehmer-xorshift RNG for map generation, viewport culling for performance, interpolated player movement at 60fps, native caps to prevent performance cliffs, and a full 80x60 tile map per dimension. Version 3.7.0 adds a second chamber canvas for the pre-game tutorial room, plus state tracking for war levels, diplomacy relations, raid parties, world events, Edict timers, and quest states — all included in the save system. Total codebase: over 15,900 lines in one file. Works completely offline after download.
